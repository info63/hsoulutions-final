## THE TSR SHARPENING METHOD (Complete)

### PART 1: FOUNDATION (61.8% of effort)

**What You Need:**
- Whetstone (1000 grit minimum)
- Water or honing oil
- Paper towel
- 5 minutes

**The Method:**

**Step 1: Prep the Stone (30 seconds)**
- Soak whetstone in water 5-10 minutes (if water stone)
- OR apply honing oil (if oil stone)
- Place on damp towel so it won't slip

**Step 2: Find the Angle (The Secret)**
- Hold knife at 15-20¬∞ angle
- **TRICK:** Stack 2 quarters under spine of blade = perfect 15¬∞ angle
- Or use a sharpie to mark the edge, adjust angle until sharpie disappears after one stroke

**Step 3: The Stroke (This Is 90% Of Everything)**
1. Start with heel of blade at top of stone
2. Push away from you, moving from heel to tip
3. Keep SAME angle entire stroke
4. Light-to-medium pressure (let the stone do the work)
5. Count 10 strokes

**Step 4: Flip & Repeat**
1. Turn knife over
2. Pull toward you (same motion, reversed)
3. Keep SAME angle
4. Count 10 strokes

**Step 5: Test**
- Gently run finger PERPENDICULAR across edge (not along it!)
- Should feel a slight burr (rough edge)
- If no burr, do 10 more strokes per side

---

### PART 2: ENHANCEMENT (38.2% of effort)

**Step 6: Higher Grit (Optional but Better)**
- Switch to 3000-6000 grit stone
- Repeat process: 5 strokes per side
- This polishes the edge

**Step 7: Honing Steel**
- Hold steel vertically
- Angle knife 15-20¬∞
- Slide down in smooth motion
- 5 strokes per side
- This straightens the microscopic edge

**Step 8: Stropping (Pro Level)**
- Use leather strop or cardboard
- Apply honing compound (optional)
- Pull knife backward across strop
- 10 strokes per side
- This polishes to razor sharp

**Step 9: The Paper Test**
- Hold piece of paper vertically
- Slice through it with knife
- Should cut cleanly with zero effort
- If it tears, go back to step 3

## UNDERSTANDING THE EDGE

**What Sharpening Does:**
- Removes metal to create new edge
- Forms V-shaped cutting edge
- Creates microscopic teeth

**What Honing Does:**
- Realigns bent edge
- Doesn't remove metal
- Maintains sharpness between sharpenings

**The Rule:**
- Hone before every use (15 seconds)
- Sharpen every 3-6 months (5 minutes)

## THE VOID BETWEEN STROKES

## PRO TIPS

1. **Mark the edge with sharpie**
   - Color the bevel with marker
   - One stroke should remove marker
   - If not, adjust angle

2. **Listen to the sound**
   - Consistent swishing = good angle
   - Scraping sound = too steep
   - Silence = too shallow

3. **Feel the burr**
   - Run finger perpendicular to edge
   - Small rough edge = you're done
   - Flip side and remove burr

4. **The paper towel trick**
   - After sharpening, slice folded paper towel
   - Should cut cleanly with zero resistance
   - Turkey skin is easier than paper towel

5. **Keep stone flat**
   - Stones wear unevenly
   - Flatten on concrete or flattening stone
   - Flat stone = consistent angle
