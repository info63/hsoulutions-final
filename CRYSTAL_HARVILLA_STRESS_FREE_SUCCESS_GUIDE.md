## STRESS MANAGEMENT: THE 3-3-3 METHOD

### WHEN YOU FEEL OVERWHELMED (Use This Immediately)

**The 3-3-3 Technique:**

**SEE 3 Things Around You**
- Look up from your desk
- Name 3 things you can see (window, plant, pen)
- Describe them in detail (blue pen, green plant, frosted window)
- **This pulls you out of stress spiral into present moment**

**FEEL 3 Things**
- Notice 3 physical sensations (feet on floor, hands on desk, back against chair)
- Press your feet into ground, feel the solid support
- **This grounds you in your body, stops panic**

**BREATHE 3 Times (Slowly)**
- Inhale 4 seconds (count: 1-2-3-4)
- Hold 4 seconds
- Exhale 4 seconds
- Repeat 3 times
- **This activates calm nervous system, shuts off stress response**

**Total Time: 2 minutes**
**Result: Stress drops 50%, clarity returns, you can think again**

**Use it:**
- Before difficult meeting
- After angry phone call
- When you want to cry/scream/quit
- End of stressful day before going home (don't bring work stress home)

## EMAIL MANAGEMENT: STOP DROWNING IN YOUR INBOX

### THE PROBLEM: Email Controls Your Day

**Sound Familiar?**
- Email open all day, ding every 3 minutes
- Interrupt deep work to answer "quick question"
- End of day: 50+ unread emails
- Anxiety: "What am I missing?"

**The Truth:**
- Average office worker checks email 36√ó per day
- Each interruption = 23 minutes to refocus
- You're spending 3-4 hours/day just SWITCHING between email and work
- **Email is not your job - it's a tool FOR your job**

---

### THE SOLUTION: Batch Processing

**New Email Rules:**

**1. Check Email 3√ó Per Day ONLY**
- 10:00 AM (during reactive block)
- 1:30 PM (during reactive block)
- 4:00 PM (during wrap-up)
- **That's it. No other times.**

**2. Close Email Outside Those Times**
- Literally close the program/tab
- Turn off notifications (ding = cortisol = stress)
- If urgent, people will call or walk over

**3. Process Email in Batches (30 min max)**
- Skim all, handle in order of importance
- Use 4-folder system:
  - **DO** (requires action from you - move to calendar/to-do list)
  - **DELEGATE** (forward with clear request)
  - **DECIDE LATER** (needs thought - schedule time to decide)
  - **DONE** (archive, you're finished)
- Goal: Every email gets ONE touch (read, decide, move on)

**4. Use Templates for Common Emails**
- "Here's the budget timeline" (you send this 20√ó per year - save template)
- "Meeting minutes attached" (template)
- "Request for public records" (template with legal language)
- **Saves 10-15 min per email**

**Result:**
- Inbox zero by end of each batch (massive stress relief)
- 90 min/day on email vs. 3-4 hours
- You control email, email doesn't control you

## MEETING MANAGEMENT: STOP WASTING TIME

### THE PROBLEM: Meetings Eat Your Life

**Your Reality:**
- Borough assembly meetings (long, political, draining)
- Staff meetings (could've been an email)
- Budget meetings (necessary but endless)
- "Quick sync" meetings (never quick)

**The Cost:**
- 10 meetings/week √ó 1 hour each = 10 hours (25% of your work week)
- Add prep time + follow-up = 15 hours (37% of your week)
- **You have 23 hours left for actual work**

---

### THE SOLUTION: Ruthless Meeting Rules

**1. Every Meeting Needs:**
- **Clear purpose** (Why are we meeting? What do we need to decide?)
- **Agenda** (sent 24 hours before)
- **Time limit** (30 min, 45 min, 60 min MAX - never "open-ended")
- **Action items** (who's doing what by when)

**If no agenda, decline the meeting.**

**2. Crystal's Meeting Rules:**

**Accept:**
- Borough assembly meetings (required)
- 1-on-1 with your boss (critical for your job)
- Budget/compliance meetings (legal requirement)

**Decline/Delegate:**
- Meetings where you're not essential (send someone else or ask for notes)
- "FYI" meetings (ask for email summary instead)
- Meetings with no agenda (reply: "Happy to attend once we have agenda")

**Shorten:**
- "1-hour meeting" ‚Üí Can this be 30 min?
- "Weekly meeting" ‚Üí Can this be every other week?
- "In-person meeting" ‚Üí Can this be a phone call?

**3. Control Meetings You Run:**

**Start on time, end on time**
- Start at :00 or :30 (9:00, 9:30, not 9:17)
- Set timer on phone
- At time limit: "We're at time. Here's what we decided. Next steps are X, Y, Z. Thanks everyone."

**Stick to agenda**
- Off-topic discussion? "That's important, let's schedule separate time for that."
- One person dominating? "Thanks, John. Let's hear from others."

**End with action items**
- "Sarah will send budget draft by Friday."
- "Mike will call contractor by Wednesday."
- "Crystal will update the form and email by Monday."
- Everyone knows their job = no confusion, no follow-up meetings

**Result:**
- Meetings drop from 15 hrs/week to 8 hrs/week (save 7 hours!)
- Meetings you DO attend are productive
- You're seen as efficient, professional

## SAYING "NO" WITHOUT FEELING GUILTY

### THE PROBLEM: You Say "Yes" to Everything

**Why You Do It:**
- You're helpful (it's your nature)
- You're good at your job (people know you'll do it right)
- You don't want to disappoint people
- You worry: "If I don't do it, who will?"

**What Happens:**
- Your to-do list explodes
- You stay late, skip lunch, work weekends
- Your actual job (the important stuff) gets pushed aside
- You're exhausted, resentful, burned out

**The Truth:**
- **Every "yes" to someone else is a "no" to yourself (your time, energy, priorities)**
- You can't do everything
- Trying to do everything = doing nothing well

---

### THE SOLUTION: Strategic "No"

**The Scripts (Use These Exactly):**

**1. "I'd love to help, but I'm at capacity right now."**
- Honest, professional, no elaboration needed
- Use for: Extra projects, volunteer requests, non-essential meetings

**2. "That's not in my wheelhouse. Have you tried [person/department]?"**
- Redirect to appropriate person
- Use for: Requests outside your role

**3. "I can do X, but not Y. Which matters more?"**
- Offer alternative, let them choose
- Use for: When you want to help but can't do it all
- Example: "I can review the budget OR attend the meeting, but not both this week. Which is priority?"

**4. "Not right now, but I can help next week."**
- Delay, not decline
- Gives you control over timing
- Use for: Legitimate requests that can wait

**5. "Let me check my workload and get back to you."**
- Buys time to think
- Prevents instant "yes" you'll regret
- Check your calendar, check with boss, then decide

**Practice:**
- Say these OUT LOUD in car/shower
- First few times will feel HARD (guilt, fear of judgment)
- After 3-5 times, it gets easier
- After 20 times, it's automatic

**What Happens:**
- People respect your boundaries
- They ask less (because they know you'll say no if you need to)
- When you DO say yes, they know you mean it
- **You protect your time = you do better work = you're more valuable**

## MANAGING DIFFICULT PEOPLE (Without Losing Your Mind)

### THE PROBLEM: People Are Stressful

**Your Reality:**
- Angry residents (taxes, permits, complaints)
- Demanding elected officials (want things NOW)
- Passive-aggressive coworkers (undermine, don't help)
- Know-it-alls (explain your job to you)

**What You Want to Say:**
- "Figure it out yourself."
- "That's not my problem."
- "You're being unreasonable."

**What You CAN'T Say** (because professionalism)

---

### THE SOLUTION: The 3-Step De-Escalation Method

**Step 1: ACKNOWLEDGE (Show You Heard Them)**
- "I hear that you're frustrated about X."
- "I understand this is important to you."
- "That sounds really challenging."
- **NOT agreeing, just reflecting**

**Why It Works:**
- People want to be HEARD
- Once they feel heard, they calm down 50%
- You're not solving yet, just listening

**Step 2: CLARIFY (Get Facts, Not Emotion)**
- "Let me make sure I understand. You're saying X?"
- "What specifically needs to happen to resolve this?"
- "When did this issue start?"
- **Ask questions, gather info**

**Why It Works:**
- Shifts from emotion to problem-solving
- You take control of conversation (calm, professional)
- Often they realize issue is smaller than they thought

**Step 3: SOLVE (Offer Next Step)**
- "Here's what I can do: X."
- "I'll look into this and get back to you by [date]."
- "That requires [boss/council/state], so here's the process..."
- **Be clear about what you CAN and CAN'T do**

**Why It Works:**
- Gives them hope (you're helping)
- Sets expectations (prevents "Why haven't you fixed this yet?!")
- Ends conversation productively

---

### EXAMPLE: ANGRY RESIDENT CALL

**Them:** "I've called FIVE TIMES about this permit and NO ONE has called me back! This is ridiculous!"

**You (Step 1 - Acknowledge):** "I hear how frustrating that must be. I'm sorry you haven't gotten a response."

**Them:** (Still mad but slightly calmer) "Yeah, I need this permit to start my project!"

**You (Step 2 - Clarify):** "Let me pull up your file. Can you give me your name and the permit number?"

**Them:** "John Smith, permit #12345."

**You:** (Looking it up) "Okay, I see here it was submitted last week. The delay is because we're waiting on the state fire marshal approval. That typically takes 10 business days."

**Them:** "Well no one TOLD me that!"

**You (Step 3 - Solve):** "You're right, we should have communicated that better. Here's what I'll do: I'll email you today with the timeline and the fire marshal's contact info so you can follow up directly. I'll also flag this for our weekly status meeting. Can I reach you at the phone number on file?"

**Them:** "Uh, yeah. Okay. Thanks."

**Result:**
- Angry person ‚Üí Calm person in 3 minutes
- You didn't promise miracles (you can't control state fire marshal)
- You gave them ACTION (email, timeline, contact info)
- You protected yourself (documented what you did)
- You stayed CALM (no stress, no taking it personally)

## ENERGY MANAGEMENT: STOP RUNNING ON EMPTY

### THE PROBLEM: You're Exhausted All the Time

### THE SOLUTION: Energy Optimization

**1. SLEEP: Non-Negotiable 7-8 Hours**

**Why It Matters:**
- Sleep = when your brain recharges
- Less than 7 hours = impaired judgment (same as being drunk)
- Chronic poor sleep = weight gain, illness, depression

**How to Get Better Sleep:**
- **Same bedtime every night** (yes, even weekends) - 10:30 PM
- **No screens 1 hour before bed** (blue light kills melatonin = can't fall asleep)
- **Room: Cool, Dark, Quiet**
  - 65-68¬∞F (Alaska winters = easy!)
  - Blackout curtains (especially summer when sun barely sets)
  - White noise machine OR fan
- **Wind-Down Routine** (tells brain: time to sleep)
  - 9:30 PM: Put phone on charger IN DIFFERENT ROOM (critical!)
  - 10:00 PM: Shower, skincare, comfy clothes
  - 10:15 PM: Read (fiction, not work stuff)
  - 10:30 PM: Lights out

**If You Can't Fall Asleep:**
- Don't force it (lying there stressed makes it worse)
- Get up, read for 20 min, try again
- 3-3-3 breathing (from earlier)
- No phone! (scroll = wake up brain)

---

**2. FOOD: Eat for Energy, Not Just Convenience**

**Your Current Diet** (Guess):
- Skip breakfast OR grab muffin/donut
- Snack on candy/chips at desk
- Lunch = whatever's fast (sandwich, leftover pizza)
- Dinner = exhausted, order takeout or cereal
- **Result: Blood sugar roller coaster = energy crash = more stress**

**Better Approach:**

**Breakfast (20 min prep):**
- Protein + Complex carbs
- Examples: Eggs + toast, Greek yogurt + granola, oatmeal + peanut butter
- **NOT:** Just coffee, sugary cereal, pastry
- **Why:** Stabilizes blood sugar, keeps you full until lunch

**Mid-Morning Snack** (if needed):
- Apple + cheese, nuts, protein bar
- **NOT:** Candy, chips, soda

**Lunch (Meal Prep on Sunday):**
- Make 5 lunches, store in fridge, grab-and-go
- Formula: Protein (chicken, turkey, beans) + Veggie + Carb (rice, quinoa, bread)
- Example: Turkey wrap with veggies, side of fruit
- **Eat AWAY from desk** (even 15 min break = mental reset)

**Afternoon Snack:**
- When 2 PM crash hits: Protein, not sugar
- Handful of almonds, string cheese, hard-boiled egg
- **NOT:** Candy bar (crashes you harder in 30 min)

**Dinner:**
- Simple = sustainable
- Rotate 3-5 easy meals (spaghetti, tacos, stir-fry, grilled chicken + veggies, chili)
- Cook once, eat leftovers 2√ó (less work!)

**Hydration:**
- 64+ oz water/day
- Keep water bottle at desk, refill 3√ó (morning, lunch, afternoon)
- Dehydration = fatigue, brain fog, stress
