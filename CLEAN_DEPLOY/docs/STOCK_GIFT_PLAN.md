## üí° TAX IMPLICATIONS

### For Shannon (Giver):
- **Gift Tax:** None (all gifts under $18k annual exclusion)
- **Income Tax:** No deduction for gifts
- **Capital Gains:** None (not a sale)

### For Recipients:
- **Income Tax:** None at time of gift (shares have nominal value)
- **Future Tax:** When they sell shares, they pay capital gains on appreciation
  - Cost basis = $0.01 per share
  - If shares worth $10 later, they pay tax on $9.99 gain per share

### Example:
- Bella receives 500,000 shares at $0.01 = $5,000 basis
- In 5 years, Liv app successful, shares worth $10 each
- Bella's shares now worth $5,000,000
- If she sells, she owes capital gains tax on $4,995,000 gain
- Long-term capital gains rate ‚âà 15-20%

1. **Alaska LLC Rules:**
   - Members can own "membership units" (like stock)
   - Operating Agreement defines ownership %
   - No stock certificates required (but recommended)
   - Transfer restrictions allowed

2. **Gift Tax:**
   - $18,000 per person per year exclusion (2024)
   - All these gifts under limit = no tax
   - Lifetime exemption: $13.61M (2024)

3. **Securities Laws:**
   - Private company = exempt from SEC registration
   - Can't publicly sell shares
   - Can transfer to family/close friends
   - If company goes public later, shares become restricted

4. **Voting Rights:**
   - Operating Agreement should specify voting
   - Recommend: Shannon retains majority vote (70%)
