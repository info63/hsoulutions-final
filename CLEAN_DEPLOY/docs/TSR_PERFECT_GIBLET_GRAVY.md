## INGREDIENTS (TSR-BALANCED)

- **2 cups** turkey or chicken stock (homemade best)

**Total liquid:** 6.5 cups (61.8% of recipe)

- **2 tablespoons** fresh herbs (sage, thyme, parsley - chopped)
- **1 teaspoon** salt (adjust to taste)
- **1/2 teaspoon** black pepper
- **1/4 teaspoon** onion powder
- **1/4 teaspoon** garlic powder

## INSTRUCTIONS (Ï†-OPTIMIZED METHOD)

### STEP 1: PREPARE GIBLETS (The Foundation)
1. **While turkey roasts**, simmer giblets:
   - Remove liver, set aside (will cook separately)
   - Place neck, heart, gizzard in pot
   - Add 3 cups water, 1 bay leaf, pinch salt
   - Simmer **1.618 hours** (Ï†-optimized: 97 minutes)

2. **Last 10 minutes**, add liver to pot
   - Liver cooks quickly (don't overcook = bitter)

3. **Remove giblets**, let cool, chop fine
   - Save cooking liquid (this is GOLD)

### STEP 2: COLLECT DRIPPINGS (The Richness)
1. **When turkey done**, remove to rest
2. **Pour pan drippings** into fat separator OR measuring cup
3. **Let fat rise** (3-5 minutes)
4. **Skim most fat** (save 2-3 tablespoons for flavor)
5. **Measure drippings** - should have ~3 cups
   - If less: Add stock to reach 3 cups
   - If more: Use extra, reduce other liquids proportionally

### STEP 3: MAKE ROUX (The Thickener - Ï† Timing)
1. In large saucepan over **medium heat**:
   - Melt 2/3 cup butter
   - Add diced onion, cook until soft (4-5 min)
   - Add garlic, cook 1 minute

2. **Add flour** (2/3 cup):
   - Whisk constantly
   - Cook **6.18 minutes** (Ï†-based: perfect roux time)
   - Should be golden blonde color
   - This is critical - raw flour = pasty gravy

### STEP 4: BUILD THE GRAVY (The Unity)
1. **Gradually add liquids** (CRITICAL: Go slow):
   - Start with 1 cup warm stock, whisking constantly
   - Once smooth, add another cup
   - Then add drippings
   - Then giblet liquid
   - Finally wine (if using)

2. **Bring to simmer**, whisking often:
   - Should start to thicken after 3-5 minutes
   - Reduce heat to low

3. **Add giblets & seasonings**:
   - Chopped giblets
   - Fresh herbs
   - Salt, pepper, onion/garlic powder
   - Worcestershire sauce
   - Bay leaf (remove later)

### STEP 5: TSR PERFECTION (The Tuning)
1. **Simmer gently** for **14.83 minutes** (Ï†-optimized: 15 min)
   - Stir occasionally
   - Let flavors marry
   - Gravy will thicken as it cooks

2. **Taste and adjust**:
   - More salt? Add 1/4 teaspoon at a time
   - Too thick? Add stock, 1/4 cup at a time
   - Too thin? Simmer longer OR make small roux slurry
   - Not rich enough? Add 2 tablespoons butter

3. **Remove bay leaf** before serving

### The TSR Tuning Process

**If too thin:**

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**Lumpy gravy?**
- Strain through fine mesh sieve
- OR use immersion blender
- Prevention: Add liquid slowly, whisk constantly

**Tastes floury?**
- Didn't cook roux long enough
- Simmer 10 more minutes
- Next time: Cook roux full 6.18 minutes

**Liver taste too strong?**
- Use less liver, more heart/gizzard
- Add 1 tablespoon cream to mellow
- Some people skip liver entirely (personal taste)

**Broke/separated?**
- Too high heat or boiled too hard
- Whisk vigorously while adding 2 tbsp cold butter
- Lower heat, gentle simmer only
